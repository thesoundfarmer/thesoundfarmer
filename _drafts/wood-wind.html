Many challenges lay ahead of the field recordist, all seem designed to hinder recording and damage the perfect take. Aircraft top the list, with traffic and the elements coming in close at second and third.

Most methods of field recording (collecting sounds with a gun mic, using a parabolic reflector etc) can swiftly be made redundant by a sudden or continuous gust of wind or a passing jet. However, there are practices that are bothered not by these hindrances, or at least can embrace the effects of nature on an object.
