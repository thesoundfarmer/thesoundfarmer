"A Bird On A Feeder, Feeding"
Recorded with Sony ECM77-B to MBox 2 to Pro Tools LE8

After attending the "In The Field" symposium at the British Library in February 2013, I'd been inspired to try out a wider range of recording techniques. Simon Elliott's methods of using wired lapel mics to record the bird's perspective were particularly exciting.

I made similar recordings a few years ago with spaced omni's but this was a bit different. I was able to leave the mic for a long period of time without leaving a recorder exposed to the elements or be attacked by wasps. I was able to run an XLR into the house, plug it straight into an MBox2, press record and go about my business with the sounds of the garden playing indoors.

As this was a test recording and I had lots of other work to be getting on with I was unable to sit and identify the various visitors to the feeder. However, recently we've been visited by a number of Sparrows, a pair of Robins, two pairs of Blackbirds, Wood Pigeons, a Great Spotted Woodpecker, a Songthrush, Blue, Great and Long Tail Tits, a Chaffinch and a squirrel, so really it could be any of those (although I am disregarding the squirrel in this case.).
